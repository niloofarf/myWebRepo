person About Me
Welcome to my website! My name is Niloofar Fathi, and I have an M.Sc. in Software Engineering.
I have knowledge and experience in both Software Security and User Experience. My background is divided into two parts: First part started after my bachelor’s degree, in which I worked in Software Security for several years. The second part started when I was working on my master’s thesis and the years following, in which I worked on User Experience and Usability, and this part has continued till now. During this, I researched various topics in User Experience, conducted several lab experiments, designed questionnaires, directed interviews, analysis of UX data, and design/re-design several UIs. Moreover, I also have valuable experiences in working on software design and software development both individually and as a team member.
Currently, I am interested in working first on UI/UX design and then on front-end programming. Although part of my previous experience is on software security, I believe it helps me in designing better user experiences by seeing the problem from the two perspectives and striking a balance between them when they are contradictory.

Intelligent Panel
date_range 2014 - 2017
This project was started when I began to work on my master's thesis and continued afterward. It consisted of two parts of research and development. Our goal was to find a solution for feature-rich applications, such as Photoshop and 3Ds Max, to improve their user experience. The final solution was an intelligent panel to predict the next command of users. I designed a prediction algorithm capable of predicting by 89% accurate according to a given dataset. I also conducted a pilot lab study with 10 subjects and a final Lab study with 30 participants. The latter experiment had two-hours long sessions including observation of user tasks, using the think-aloud method, interviewing users, and a few questionnaires. Our results showed an improvement in users' performance and an increase in users' satisfaction, a decrease in users’ cognitive load, and better feeling toward the new application.
